Hard Style Rules

The non-negotiables.

These aren't suggestions. Write to one real, singular reader — use "you" not "players", and speak from "we". Refer to The New Oxford Style Manual for British spelling conventions.

✅ Always

→Safeplay (not Safe Play)
→Signup (not sign-up)
→90-ball (not 90 ball)
→Dab (not daub)
→Contractions — "don't" not "do not"
→British spelling conventions
→Specific numbers, not vague language
→Use "if" for slot features, not "when"
→Respond as "we" — never "I"

🚫 Never

→"Bankroll" — not a British term
→"Daub" — use "dab"
→Vague numbers ("a few", "some")
→Hype language or overpromising
→AI-sounding or generic copy — if it could have been written for any brand, rewrite it
→Guaranteed win language or anything suggesting a win is due
→Promos in specific detail — keep copy top-level and general. Detailed promo copy triggers a legal requirement to include full T&Cs. (We can add T&Cs for welcome offers, but we don't want every page covered in them.)
→"Bonus hunters will love this" — we don't want to encourage players to join a site purely to claim a bonus
→"When" for slot features — always use "if". Never suggest a win is due or certain if a player keeps playing
→Responding as an individual ("I") — always "we"

📏 On Length

→Length guidance is always in your copy brief
→No brief? Shorter is almost always better
→Emails: each line earns the next
→Socials: short, conversational, no waffle
→CTAs: lead with a verb — keep it punchy
Responsible Gambling Context

Know the rules. Write responsibly.

UKGC Stake Limits

The UKGC has set a max stake limit on all online slots: £5 per spin for over 25s and £2 per spin for 18–24 year olds. Even if a game supports higher bets, UK-licensed operators are legally required to cap this. Always write with this context in mind.

Always Encourage Safe Play

Remind players to stay within their limits. Recommend our safer gambling guide. Encourage use of Safeplay tools. Never use language that implies chasing losses is acceptable. Use "if" not "when" for slot features.

Handling Difficult Situations

How we respond when it matters most.

Every response represents WhichBingo — not you as an individual. Always respond as "we" or "the WhichBingo team."

❌ Wrong

"That's an issue with the operator, not us. We just review sites. You need to contact them directly."

✅ Right

"Hello, I'm the WhichBingo Admin. We're really sorry to hear you're experiencing delays. We completely understand how frustrating slow payouts can be. While we review and rate operators, we don't have direct control over individual casino operations. The best route is to escalate directly with the operator's support. Could you let us know how long you've been waiting? Your experience helps other players make informed choices."

❌ Wrong

"We can't do anything about your account. Contact the casino."

✅ Right

"We understand your frustration — account issues are incredibly stressful. Just to clarify, we're WhichBingo — we review and rate bingo halls and casinos, but we don't have access to operator accounts. Your best course of action is to contact the operator's support team directly. Please keep us updated — experiences like yours help inform our reviews."

❌ Wrong

"There's no need to be rude. We're just doing our job."

✅ Right

"We can see you're really frustrated, and that's completely understandable. We're here to help in any way we can. [Address their concern with empathy and clarity.] We're on your side — our whole purpose is to make playing safer and clearer for everyone."

❌ Wrong

"Our reviews are thoroughly researched. Perhaps you misread something. We can't update every review constantly."

✅ Right

"Thank you so much for bringing this to our attention. You're absolutely right that this information needs updating. That's on us, and we apologise. We're updating the review now and it should be live within 24 hours. Your feedback helps us maintain the accuracy our community depends on."

❌ Wrong

"We are completely independent. This accusation is unfair and untrue."

✅ Right

"We completely understand why you might question that — trust is everything in this space. To be transparent: WhichBingo operates independently and is not owned by any casino or operator. We do earn commission when players sign up through our links, but this doesn't influence our ratings or reviews. We know we have to earn your trust through our actions, not just our words."

❌ Wrong

"We get hundreds of messages. We responded as quickly as we could."

✅ Right

"We're really sorry. You reached out to us for help and we let you down by not responding quickly enough. That's not the service we want to provide, and it's not fair to you. [Address their actual concern.] Thank you for giving us another chance to help. We'll do better."

The Golden Rules

For every difficult response.

Can't tick all five? Rewrite it.

💛

Lead with empathy

"We understand," "We're sorry to hear," "That must be frustrating"

🏷️

Identify as WhichBingo

Always "WhichBingo Admin" or "the WhichBingo team"

🔦

Clarify our role

Explain patiently what we do and don't control

🛠️

Offer what we CAN do

Always give something useful, even if it's just guidance

🤝

End supportively

"We hope this gets resolved" — leave the door open

"Own it. Fix it. Learn from it."

When we mess up, our response is a chance to show our values in action.

Channel Guidelines

Same brand. Right format.

The voice stays consistent across every channel. Specific length guidance is always in your individual copy brief.

UX Copy

No sales or marketing lingo. Guide the user clearly. Add wit when it entertains without distracting. Don't overuse CTAs.

Goal: clarity first, personality second.
📏 Length: per copy brief

Emails

Get straight to the point. Every line earns the next. Always think about the mood your audience will be in.

Goal: earn every sentence.
📏 Length: per copy brief

Socials

Be the friend who actually plays. Empathetic if frustrated, celebratory if winning, informative if confused. Short, conversational, fair.

Goal: short, human, real.
📏 Length: per copy brief

CTAs

Lead with verbs. "Find out", "Compare", "Check". Never passive, never vague.

Goal: action, not decoration.
📏 Length: punchy — per copy brief

News Articles

Know your message and stick to it. Add useful links. Keep SEO in mind. Fun for pop culture — factual for regulation topics.

Fun where it fits. Factual where it counts.
📏 Length: per copy brief

Comments & Reviews

Casual, empathetic and smart. Respond with empathy, not aggression. Always clarify who we are.

"Hello, I'm the WhichBingo Admin..."
📏 Length: per copy brief
Before You Publish

The final check.

For all copy:
  • ✓ Does it hit at least 3 of 5 in the voice formula?
  • ✓ Would your nan understand it?
  • ✓ Would your mate not roll their eyes?
  • ✓ Does it sound like a real person wrote it?
  • ✓ Is the tone matched to the reader's mood?
For difficult responses:
  • ✓ Does this sound understanding and compassionate?
  • ✓ Have I explained our role without being defensive?
  • ✓ Am I speaking as WhichBingo ("we"), not myself ("I")?
  • ✓ Would I want to receive this if I was frustrated?
  • ✓ Does this uphold our values: transparent, reliable, trusted, community?
