⚠ Compliance — Read This First

Non-negotiable. Every time.

Two things in particular will get copy rejected — or cause legal problems. Know these before you write a word.

🎰 Responsible Gambling Language

→Always use "if" not "when" for slot features. Never suggest a win is due or certain.
→Never use language that implies chasing losses is acceptable.
→Never guarantee wins or suggest outcomes.
→Where relevant, encourage safe play and use of Safeplay tools.
→UKGC stake limits: Max £5/spin (over 25s) and £2/spin (18–24s) on all online slots. Always write with this in mind.

📋 Promotions & Bonus Copy

→Don't describe current promotions in specific detail. Detailed promo copy triggers a legal requirement to include full T&Cs — keep it top-level and general enough to stay compliant.
→(We can add T&Cs when talking about welcome offers, but we don't want every moment on every page covered in them.)
→✅ OK: "This site offers a welcome bonus for new players — check the T&Cs before signing up."
→❌ Not OK: "Get 200 free spins and a 100% match up to £200 when you deposit £20."
→If in doubt, keep it vague and link to the operator's current offers page.

Hard Style Rules

The non-negotiables.

These apply to everything you write for WhichBingo. Refer to The New Oxford Style Manual for British spelling.

✅ Always

→Safeplay (not Safe Play)
→Signup (not sign-up)
→90-ball (not 90 ball)
→Dab (not daub)
→Contractions — "don't" not "do not"
→British spelling conventions
→Specific numbers, not vague language
→Use "if" for slot features, never "when"

🚫 Never

→"Bankroll" — not a British term
→"Daub" — use "dab"
→Vague numbers ("a few", "some")
→Hype language or overpromising
→Generic copy that could apply to any brand — if it reads like ChatGPT wrote it, rewrite it
→Guaranteed win language or anything suggesting a win is due
→Promos in specific detail — keep copy top-level and general. Detailed promo copy triggers a legal requirement to include full T&Cs. (We can add T&Cs for welcome offers, but we don't want every page covered in them.)
→"Bonus hunters will love this" — we don't want to encourage players to join a site purely to claim a bonus
→"When" for slot features — always use "if". Never suggest a win is due if a player keeps playing

📏 On Length

→Length guidance is always in your copy brief — check it before you start
→No brief guidance? Shorter is almost always better
→Be specific — avoid padding to hit a word count
→Every sentence should earn its place
Channel Specifics

Your three content types.

The voice stays consistent. Here's how it applies to the work you'll actually be doing.

📰 News Articles

Know what you want to write and stick to the message. Add useful links. Keep SEO best practices in mind throughout.

→Pop culture or light player stories: have fun. Witty headlines, playful turns of phrase.
→Regulation, licensing, responsible gambling: measured, respectful, factual. No levity.
→Fun where it fits. Factual where it counts.
📏 Length: always in your brief

🖥 UX Copy

The goal is to guide the user clearly. Clarity always comes before entertainment.

→No sales or marketing lingo — ever.
→Add wit only when it entertains without distracting.
→CTAs lead with verbs: "Find out", "Compare", "Check". Never passive.
→Don't overuse CTAs — guide, don't push.
📏 Length: always in your brief

🔍 SEO / Landing Pages

Optimise for search without losing the voice. The reader still comes first — write for them, not just the algorithm.

→Promotions compliance is especially critical here — keep bonus copy top-level only.
→Specific over vague, always — for copy and for SEO.
→Give readers real opinions, not just information. We guide — we don't list facts.
→If it could appear on any bingo site, it's not WhichBingo yet. Rewrite it.
📏 Length: always in your brief
